# 📊 Retail Analysis Sales Dashboard

## Overview  
This Power BI dashboard provides a clear view of district-level sales performance. It helps track monthly trends, compare year-over-year results, and analyze product category performance across stores and managers.

The report is designed for sales teams and business stakeholders who need quick, reliable insights to support decisions.

## Tools & Technologies  

- **Power BI Desktop** – Dashboard development and visualization  
- **Power Query** – Data cleaning and transformation  
- **DAX** – Measures for KPIs such as Sales, Variance %, and Avg $/Unit  
- **Data Modeling** – Relationships between sales, date, store, and category tables  

## Data  

The dataset contains retail sales data, including:  
- Store and district information  
- Monthly sales figures  
- Product categories (Women, Men, Kids, Shoes, etc.)  
- Pricing and unit metrics  



## Key Features  

### 1. District Filtering  
A slicer allows users to view performance by individual district managers.

### 2. Sales by Store  
Bar chart showing current-year sales across stores, making it easy to identify top and low performers.

### 3. Monthly Sales Variance (%)  
Displays percentage change in sales by month to highlight growth or decline.

### 4. Year-over-Year Comparison  
Area chart comparing last year vs. this year to show trends and seasonality.

### 5. Category Performance  
Bubble chart analyzing:  
- Sales variance (%)  
- Average price per unit  
- Category contribution

## Insights You Can Get  

- Identify which districts and stores are performing well  
- Track monthly sales trends and seasonality  
- Compare current performance against last year  
- Understand which product categories drive revenue  
- Spot declines early and take action

## How to Use  

1. Open the `.pbix` file in Power BI Desktop  
2. Use the **District Manager slicer** to filter results  
3. Hover over visuals for detailed metrics  
4. Explore category and monthly trends for deeper insights
